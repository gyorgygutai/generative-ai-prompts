## Prompt

a photorealistic high-fashion editorial portrait in a vertical 4:5 frame, cropped tightly from the top of the head to mid-waist. Keep the composition intimate and close, with the subject filling most of the frame and very little dead space. Shoot at eye level with a subtle three-quarter turn through the shoulders so the portrait feels immediate and self-possessed rather than flat.

Place her in a bright modern hallway beside a large window on the left side of the frame, with a neutral beige wall, clean architectural lines, and an uncluttered softly blurred background. The environment should feel real, quiet, and expensive without looking staged or decorative.

Style her in a fitted white ribbed tank with visible knit texture and clean edge finish, layered under a light-wash vintage denim jacket shrugged low off the shoulders. The denim should feel substantial and worn-in, with believable weight, faded wash variation, soft abrasion at the edges, natural seam structure, and heavy folds where it collapses around the upper arms and torso. Let the jacket drape be intentional and asymmetrical rather than evenly styled. Add only small gold hoops and a thin gold chain necklace. Optional amber-tinted micro cat-eye sunglasses may sit low on the nose as a sharp editorial accent, but they must never hide the eyes.

Build the pose around one hand lightly pinching the denim collar near the collarbone, creating clear tension through the jacket fold and shoulder line. The opposite arm stays lower and more relaxed, with a natural bend at the elbow to avoid symmetry. Posture tall and composed. Expression calm, direct, and faintly knowing, with a slight smirk rather than a smile. Eyes locked to camera.

Hair should remain fully consistent with the reference while styled into a sleek low bun with two face-framing strands and a few realistic flyaways. Makeup should stay soft-glam but believable: defined brows, subtle warmth in the cheeks, glossy nude lips, clean lash definition, and realistic skin with visible pores, faint freckles, tonal variation, and natural micro-asymmetry.

Light the portrait with strong directional window light from the left, shaping the face with a clean shadow gradient across the far cheek and jaw. Keep highlights controlled on the cheekbones and lip gloss, with clear catchlights in the eyes and no flash look. The background should stay cooler and softer than the skin, with gentle falloff and no blown highlights.

Use a real 50mm portrait feel with shallow depth of field, keeping the eyes, nearest facial plane, denim collar tension, and rib texture sharply resolved while the background falls softly away. Preserve realistic hands and fingers, accurate denim seams, natural anatomy, subtle film-like contrast, and crisp detail without HDR harshness.

The final image should feel sharp, intimate, and editorial, built from window light, shoulder-line asymmetry, worn denim structure, and steady eye contact rather than generic clean-girl portrait polish.
